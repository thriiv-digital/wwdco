Platform Selection

Shopify vs WooCommerce — Which Is Right for Your Wollongong Business?

Both are excellent platforms for different reasons. We're not tied to either — we recommend based on your catalogue, your team's technical comfort, and your growth plans.

Shopify
Hosted Platform

Best for businesses that want lower maintenance overhead

  • Fully hosted — no server management or security patches
  • Built-in payment processing (Shopify Payments)
  • Excellent mobile app for managing orders on the go
  • App ecosystem for extending functionality
  • Faster to launch, lower upfront development cost
  • POS integration for businesses with a physical store
  • Predictable monthly cost (plan + apps)
Best for: retailers, product businesses, businesses new to eCommerce, those wanting simplicity over flexibility.
WooCommerce
WordPress Plugin

Best for businesses that want maximum flexibility and control

  • Complete ownership — your server, your data, your store
  • No transaction fees beyond payment processor
  • Unlimited customisation — any design, any functionality
  • Deeper integration with WordPress content and SEO
  • Better for complex catalogues with custom product types
  • Lower long-term cost for high-volume stores
  • Requires active maintenance (updates, security)
Best for: businesses already on WordPress, complex catalogues, subscription products, or those wanting full long-term control.

Our recommendation: For most Wollongong businesses starting out or relaunching, Shopify is the right choice. For established businesses with existing WordPress sites, complex product requirements, or subscription models, WooCommerce is usually the better fit. We'll advise during your discovery call after understanding your specific situation.

What We Build Into Every Store

eCommerce Features That Drive Sales, Not Just Visits

Every store we build comes with the features and optimisations that separate high-converting stores from expensive digital brochures.

Mobile-first checkout

Over half of all eCommerce purchases happen on a phone. We design every store mobile-first — tap targets, payment flow, product images, and form fields all optimised for thumb navigation and small screens.

Speed-optimised build

Slow stores lose customers. We compress every image, defer non-critical scripts, use lazy loading, and run every store through Google PageSpeed before launch. Sub-3-second loads on 4G are our baseline.

💳

Australian payment gateways

Stripe, PayPal, Afterpay, Zip Pay — the payment methods Australians trust and expect. We configure all gateways, test every checkout flow, and optimise for minimum cart abandonment at the payment step.

🔍

eCommerce SEO structure

Category pages, product pages, breadcrumbs, structured data, canonical URLs, and sitemap configuration all set up to Google's best practice specifications from launch day. Your products start ranking faster.

Abandoned cart recovery

Typically 70% of shoppers who add to cart don't complete the purchase. We configure automated cart recovery emails with your brand voice, sent at the optimal intervals to bring customers back to complete their order.

Shipping configuration

Flat rate, free shipping thresholds, weight-based rates, real-time carrier rates, Australia Post integration — we configure your shipping rules correctly from the start so customers see accurate costs and you don't lose margin.

Product reviews & social proof

Strategy

eCommerce Strategy & Platform Recommendation

Before we open a design file, we spend time understanding your business: your product catalogue, your target customer, your competitors, your fulfilment process, your growth ambitions, and your team's technical capacity. eCommerce decisions made without this context — choosing the wrong platform, building a catalogue structure that doesn't scale, missing key integrations — are expensive to fix later.

We help you think through the questions that matter before build: Should you sell on your own store, a marketplace, or both? Do you need subscription billing? What does your warehouse or fulfilment workflow look like, and how does that connect to your store? Do you have existing wholesale customers who need a separate portal? What's the post-launch marketing plan — are you relying on SEO, Ads, social, or some combination?

The strategy phase produces a clear project brief: platform selection with rationale, sitemap and catalogue structure, integration list, payment gateway selection, and a realistic timeline and budget. Everything agreed before a line of design begins.

Traffic

eCommerce SEO & Product Page Optimisation

An online store with no organic traffic is entirely dependent on paid ads to generate revenue — and that's a precarious position. eCommerce SEO is how you build a traffic base that grows month after month without paying for every visitor. It's also the single biggest point of failure for most eCommerce businesses that launch without it.

Every store we build is SEO-ready from launch day: category pages are structured with keyword-targeted headings and descriptions, product pages have unique titles and meta descriptions, product images are compressed and labelled correctly, canonical URLs prevent duplicate content issues from filtering variations, breadcrumbs are implemented with schema markup, and an XML sitemap is submitted to Google Search Console immediately post-launch.

For established stores or those in competitive categories, we offer an ongoing eCommerce SEO service: category page content expansion, product description optimisation, blog content targeting informational searches, and link building to category and product pages. This is where sustainable long-term revenue growth for eCommerce businesses is built.

FAQ

eCommerce Web Design Wollongong — Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an eCommerce website cost in Wollongong?
▾
Our eCommerce builds start at $5,500 for a Starter Store (up to 50 products on Shopify or WooCommerce) and go up to $15,000+ for large catalogues, B2B portals, or complex custom builds. The most common project for an established Wollongong retailer falls in the $9,500–$12,000 range. We provide a fixed, itemised quote after a free discovery call — no surprises.
How long does it take to build an online store?
▾
Most eCommerce builds take 8–12 weeks from kickoff to launch. Timeline depends primarily on catalogue size (importing 500 products takes longer than 50), integration complexity, and how quickly you can provide feedback during the design phase. We give you a project timeline in the proposal and update it weekly so you always know where things are at.
Should I use Shopify or WooCommerce?
▾
Shopify is usually the better choice for businesses launching their first store or those who want simplicity and lower maintenance overhead. WooCommerce is better for businesses already on WordPress, those with complex product types (subscriptions, bundles, B2B pricing), or those wanting full long-term ownership without transaction fees. We're not tied to either and will recommend based on your specific situation after a discovery call.
Can you migrate my existing online store to a new platform?
▾
Yes. We handle migrations from any platform — WooCommerce to Shopify, Magento to WooCommerce, BigCommerce to Shopify, or any other combination. A migration includes full product data transfer (titles, descriptions, images, variants, SKUs, inventory), redirect mapping for all old URLs, and Google Search Console re-submission post-migration to protect your existing organic rankings.
Do you set up Afterpay and Zip Pay?
▾
Yes — buy-now-pay-later options are standard in Australian eCommerce and we configure them on every build. Afterpay and Zip Pay are included in our standard payment gateway setup. You'll need to apply for a merchant account with each provider, which we assist with, and approval is typically fast for established businesses.
Will my store be set up for Google Shopping?
▾
Yes. Every eCommerce build includes a Google Merchant Centre account setup and a product feed connection so your products are eligible for Google Shopping and Performance Max campaigns. The Growth Store and Enterprise packages include Shopping campaign setup and initial management. For Starter Store clients, we assist with Merchant Centre setup and provide guidance on managing Shopping campaigns yourself or through our Google Ads service.
