Every year, Wollongong business owners spend thousands of dollars on websites that don't work. Not because web design is complicated — it isn't — but because they chose the wrong agency and had no framework for evaluating who they were dealing with.

I've spent my career in digital agencies. Before founding Wollongong Web Design Co, I was second-in-command at one of Australia's larger agencies, overseeing more than 600 client accounts. I've seen what happens when businesses choose agencies for the wrong reasons. And I've seen the aftermath — underperforming sites, locked-in contracts, unresponsive account managers, and businesses that spent $5,000 and still have nothing to show for it on Google.

This guide is everything I wish someone had handed to Wollongong business owners before they signed anything. It's practical, it's honest, and it's specifically written for the Illawarra market.

The Wollongong web design landscape — what you're actually dealing with

The Illawarra has a surprisingly crowded web design market given its size. You'll find local freelancers working from home, boutique agencies based in Wollongong CBD, regional agencies in Sydney claiming to service the Illawarra, and offshore providers marketing themselves as Australian. Prices range from $500 to $50,000+ for what, on paper, looks like a similar product.

The challenge for a business owner is that most web design services look identical until after you've paid. Portfolios show finished sites. Proposals use the same language. Everyone claims to do "SEO-ready" and "mobile-first" builds.

What actually separates a great agency from a mediocre one — and a mediocre one from a genuinely damaging engagement — comes down to a handful of factors that are completely visible if you know where to look. Here's what to look for.

📋 Before you start

Get at least three quotes. Not because you're looking for the cheapest, but because the process of getting quotes reveals how agencies communicate, how thorough their questions are, and whether they actually understand your business. An agency that asks good questions before quoting is an agency that builds good websites.

7 red flags that should send you running

These aren't minor concerns. Each one of these is a signal that should give you serious pause — and in some cases, end the conversation.

🚩

Red Flag #1: No local portfolio, or only template screenshots

Any agency worth hiring can show you real websites they've built for real Wollongong businesses. If their portfolio is full of template demos, stock imagery mockups, or suspiciously generic sites with no client names attached — that's a serious problem. Ask for live URLs and check that the sites actually work on mobile and load quickly.

🚩

Red Flag #2: Lock-in contracts of 12 months or more

A reputable agency doesn't need to lock you in. If an agency requires a 12- or 24-month commitment for ongoing services — SEO, maintenance, hosting — ask yourself why. Confident agencies earn your business every month. Lock-in contracts exist to protect the agency's revenue, not to deliver better results for you. Month-to-month is the standard to hold out for.

🚩

Red Flag #3: Vague pricing with no itemised breakdown

If an agency gives you a single number with no breakdown of what's included, that number will grow. "Scope creep" — where additional charges appear during the project for things you assumed were included — is one of the most common complaints about web design agencies. A professional agency can tell you exactly what is and isn't included in their quote before you sign.

🚩

Red Flag #4: SEO is an "add-on" not built into the process

A website that nobody finds is a brochure. If an agency separates "web design" from "SEO" and treats optimisation as an optional extra, they're building you a product that won't work for your business. SEO fundamentals — clean code, fast load speeds, proper heading structure, schema markup, Google Search Console setup — should be standard in every build, not an upgrade.

🚩

Red Flag #5: You can never speak to the person actually building your site

Large agencies assign account managers who relay messages to developers you never meet. This creates delays, misunderstandings, and a finished product that doesn't match what you described. The best web design relationships involve direct communication with the person writing your copy, designing your pages, and setting up your SEO. If the agency can't introduce you to that person before you sign, ask why.

🚩

Red Flag #6: They claim to be local but have never heard of Fairy Meadow

There are dozens of Sydney and Melbourne agencies running Google Ads targeting "Wollongong web design." They're not local. They don't know the difference between a Wollongong CBD search and a Thirroul search. They don't understand the seasonal patterns of the South Coast, the competitive landscape of the Illawarra trades market, or which suburbs your customers are actually coming from. Local knowledge isn't just a nice-to-have — it's a material advantage in local SEO.

🚩

Red Flag #7: They use urgency tactics or pressure you to decide quickly

"We only have one project slot left this month." "This price is only available until Friday." A legitimate agency that does good work doesn't need manufactured urgency. They have enough enquiries from word-of-mouth and referrals that they don't need to pressure anyone into signing. Urgency tactics are a sales technique — and a reliable signal that the agency relies on them because their work doesn't speak for itself.

6 green flags — what good actually looks like

Knowing what to avoid is half the battle. Here's what a genuinely strong Wollongong web design agency looks like in practice.

✅

Green Flag #1: Real case studies with measurable results

Not just pretty screenshots — actual before-and-after data. Traffic increases, ranking improvements, enquiry volumes, conversion rates. If an agency has produced results for other Wollongong businesses, they can prove it. If they can't or won't, ask yourself whether those results exist.

✅

Green Flag #2: Google Partner certification

Google Partner status means the agency has met Google's requirements for ad spend, account performance, and certified staff. It's not a guarantee of quality across all services, but it's a meaningful signal that the agency is serious about digital marketing and has passed Google's own competency checks. For businesses looking at Google Ads alongside their website, it's an important credential.

✅

Green Flag #3: Transparent, fixed pricing

A clear quote that breaks down exactly what's included — design, development, copywriting, SEO setup, testing, launch, and what ongoing services cost — is the sign of an agency that knows its process well enough to price it accurately. Fixed quotes protect you from bill shock. Month-to-month ongoing services protect you from being locked in.

✅

Green Flag #4: They ask smart questions before they quote

A good agency wants to understand your business, your customers, your competitors, and your goals before they write a number on a page. If an agency can quote you without asking what industry you're in, who your target customer is, or what you want the website to achieve — they're not building you a strategy. They're selling you a template with a logo swap.

✅

Green Flag #5: You'll own everything when it's done

Domain, hosting account, website files, code, content — all of it should be yours at the end of the project. Some agencies retain ownership of your site to maintain leverage over ongoing fees. Any reputable agency will confirm in writing that you have full ownership and can move your site anywhere you like, with no exit penalties.

✅

Green Flag #6: A post-launch plan, not just a launch date

A website is not a set-and-forget asset. An agency that thinks about what happens after launch — Google Search Console submission, indexing, ongoing SEO, performance monitoring — is an agency that understands digital. The best agencies treat launch as the start of the relationship, not the end of the project.

Local vs. national vs. offshore — which is right for a Wollongong business?

You have three basic options when choosing a web design agency, and each comes with real trade-offs.

Local Wollongong agency

Best for: Most Illawarra businesses, especially those with a local customer base or competing for local search traffic.

  • Understands the Wollongong market, local search behaviour, and regional competition
  • Available for face-to-face meetings in Wollongong, Shellharbour or Kiama
  • Faster communication in your timezone, on your schedule
  • Has a reputational stake in the Illawarra community — they can't disappear
  • Local SEO knowledge is genuine, not assumed

National Australian agency (Sydney, Melbourne)

Best for: Businesses operating nationally with no need for local SEO.

  • Often larger teams and broader capability
  • May have more experience with enterprise-level builds
  • Regional clients (like Wollongong businesses) are typically low priority
  • No genuine understanding of the Illawarra market
  • Face-to-face meetings require travel
  • Often more expensive for equivalent work

Offshore or overseas provider

Best for: Businesses with very limited budgets and very simple requirements, and only if they have the technical knowledge to manage the relationship themselves.

  • Significantly lower upfront cost
  • Time zone and communication challenges are real, not overstated
  • Zero local SEO knowledge
  • Quality is highly variable and difficult to assess before engaging
  • Revisions and post-launch support can be slow and difficult
  • No accountability if things go wrong
📌 Our honest recommendation

For any Wollongong business competing for local customers — trades, hospitality, health, professional services, retail — a local agency is almost always the better choice. The local SEO advantage alone justifies it. An agency that knows the Illawarra will target the right suburbs, the right search terms, and the right Google Business Profile strategy from day one.

10 questions to ask before you sign anything

These questions work in any meeting with a web design agency — in person, on Zoom, or via email. The quality of the answers will tell you almost everything you need to know.

1. Who will actually be building my website?

You want to know if it's the person you're talking to, a team member you'll meet, or an offshore contractor you'll never speak to. The answer matters.

2. Can you show me three live websites you've built for Wollongong or Illawarra businesses?

Not mockups. Not templates. Live, working websites with real clients you can call if you want a reference.

3. What's included in the quote — and what will cost extra?

This is where vague proposals reveal themselves. Copywriting? Photography? Schema markup? Google Search Console setup? Post-launch support? Get it in writing.

4. Will I own the domain, hosting account, and all website files when we're done?

The answer should be an unambiguous yes. If there's any hesitation or qualification, treat it as a red flag.

5. What CMS will the site be built on, and will I be able to update it myself?

WordPress, Webflow, Shopify — all are legitimate depending on your needs. What matters is that you can edit your own content without calling the agency every time you want to change a phone number.

6. How is SEO handled during the build?

A strong answer covers: keyword research informing the page structure, optimised title tags and meta descriptions, schema markup, site speed optimisation, and Google Search Console submission at launch. A weak answer is "we can add SEO later."

7. What does your post-launch process look like?

Good agencies monitor the site after launch, check for indexing issues, confirm conversions are tracking, and stay available for 30 days minimum. Agencies that disappear after handover leave you exposed.

8. Is the ongoing work (maintenance, SEO, hosting) month-to-month or locked in?

Month-to-month is the correct answer. If they require a 12-month lock-in, ask why — and be sceptical of the response.

9. How do you measure success, and what will you report on?

Vanity metrics (impressions, followers) are not success. Real metrics: organic traffic, keyword rankings, form submissions, phone calls, revenue. Ask what they track and how often they report it.

10. What happens if I'm not happy with the result?

Revision rounds, approval gates, escalation processes — how does the agency handle disagreements or unsatisfactory work? The answer tells you a lot about how they treat clients when things aren't perfect.

Contracts, ownership and the fine print

Most disputes between businesses and web design agencies come down to three things: what was agreed, who owns what, and what happens if either party wants to leave. Here's what to check before signing.

Ownership of the website

Your contract should explicitly state that upon final payment, all website files, code, design assets, and content become your property. Some agencies retain ownership of the "design" while you own the "content" — this creates ongoing leverage. Everything should transfer to you.

Domain and hosting accounts

Insist that domain registrations and hosting accounts are in your name. Agencies that register your domain or set up your hosting under their own account have leverage over your business that you don't want them to have. If you ever need to leave, you shouldn't need their cooperation to take your website with you.

Revision rounds

How many design revisions are included? What counts as a revision? "Unlimited revisions" is a red flag — it usually means low-quality initial work that requires many rounds to get right, and "unlimited" is never actually unlimited when you read the fine print.

Termination clauses

For ongoing services, what are the notice periods? What happens to your website if you cancel? Some agencies delete sites or revoke access when clients leave. A clean exit — your files, your data, with reasonable notice — is what you should be signing up for.

⚠️ Watch out for this

Some agencies build sites on proprietary platforms or using page builders that require their ongoing subscription to function. If you leave, your site breaks. Always confirm that your website can be moved to any hosting provider and will continue to function independently of the agency's tools or accounts.

The complete 10-point checklist

Use this before engaging any web design agency in Wollongong. If you can tick all 10, you're in good hands.

  • They have a portfolio of live local websites with real client names you can verify
  • They can show you measurable results — traffic, rankings, or enquiry data — from past projects
  • They asked smart questions about your business before providing a quote
  • The quote is itemised — you know exactly what is and isn't included
  • Ongoing services are month-to-month, not locked into a 12+ month contract
  • You will own the domain, hosting account, and all website files upon final payment
  • SEO fundamentals are built into the project, not sold as an add-on
  • You will have direct access to the person building your website
  • They have a clear post-launch process including Google indexing and monitoring
  • They are genuinely based in Wollongong or the Illawarra, not just claiming to be

Frequently asked questions

How do I choose a web design agency in Wollongong?

Look for a local agency with a verified portfolio, real case studies with measurable results, transparent pricing, no lock-in contracts, and direct access to the person doing the work. Google Partner certification is a strong credential. Avoid agencies that can't show local results, push long-term lock-in contracts, or outsource work offshore without telling you.

What questions should I ask a web design agency before hiring them?

Ask who will actually build your site, whether you'll own everything at the end, what's included in the quote and what costs extra, how SEO is handled during the build, and what ongoing services cost and whether they're locked in. See our full list of 10 questions above.

Should I use a local Wollongong agency or a national one?

For most Illawarra businesses, a local agency is the better choice. They understand the local market, know which search terms Wollongong customers actually use, and are available for face-to-face meetings. National agencies treat regional clients as low-priority accounts and often assign junior staff or offshore contractors.

Do I own my website after it's built?

You should — but not all agencies give you full ownership. Always confirm in writing that you own the domain, the hosting account, and all website files. Some agencies retain ownership to lock you into ongoing fees. A reputable Wollongong agency will hand everything over with no strings attached.

What's a reasonable price for a website in Wollongong?

A professional small business website built by a reputable Wollongong agency typically costs between $1,950 and $8,000. eCommerce sites start around $3,500 and go up depending on product volume and complexity. Be wary of anything significantly below this range — the savings usually come from templates, offshore developers, or work that won't rank on Google.
