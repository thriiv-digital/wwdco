You're 4 metres up a ladder at a job in Fairy Meadow. Your phone rings. You can't answer. By the time you're back on the ground 20 minutes later, the caller hasn't left a voicemail — they've already called the next plumber on the list and booked them.

That call cost you a job. And if you're honest, it happens several times a day.

Missed call text-back fixes this with zero effort on your part. It's one of the simplest, highest-ROI tools any Illawarra tradie can add to their business — and most haven't heard of it.

The problem every tradie knows too well

Tradies are among the hardest workers to reach during business hours. You're driving, operating tools, in a roof space, in a meeting, or on the phone to a supplier. Missing calls isn't a character flaw — it's physics. But the customers calling you don't see it that way. They see an unanswered phone and assume you're either too busy, disorganised, or simply not interested in their job.

Research consistently shows that 78% of customers book the first business that responds to their enquiry. If a competitor picks up and you don't — or if they send an immediate text and you return a call 3 hours later — the job is almost certainly gone. In the Wollongong tradie market, where customers often call 2-3 businesses and take the first callback, speed of response is a genuine competitive advantage.

📱 The numbers

The average tradie misses 3–8 calls per working day. At an average job value of $400–$800, even recovering one missed call per week adds $20,000–$40,000 to annual revenue. Missed call text-back costs less than $100/month.

How missed call text-back actually works

It's simpler than it sounds. When a call to your business number goes unanswered — whether you're busy, driving, on another call, or after hours — the system automatically fires an SMS to the caller's number within 30 seconds. The message is pre-written in your voice, explains you're unavailable right now, and invites them to reply with what they need.

The customer gets an instant response. They feel acknowledged. They're far more likely to wait for your callback than to call someone else — because you've already started a conversation with them.

When you're free, you check your SMS thread, see what they need, and call back prepared. No chasing voicemails, no guessing what the job is, no cold callbacks.

The technology behind it is straightforward — your business phone number is connected to an automation platform (we typically use Make or a purpose-built CRM) that monitors for missed calls and triggers the SMS. Setup takes less than a day. After that, it runs silently in the background 24/7.

Why SMS works better than voicemail

Voicemail has an open rate problem. Most people under 50 won't listen to a voicemail from a number they don't recognise — they'll call back if they feel like it, or move on. SMS open rates are 98%. The vast majority of people read a text within 3 minutes of receiving it.

Beyond open rates, SMS creates a conversation. Once a customer has replied to your text, they've invested in the interaction. They're far less likely to book someone else because they're now in a dialogue with you. It's a psychological commitment that voicemail doesn't create.

There's also a practical advantage: when you call back after a missed call, you're going in blind. When you call back after a text exchange, you already know the suburb, the type of job, any urgency, and what to bring. First impressions improve dramatically when you can say "ah yes, the kitchen tap in Bulli" rather than "uh, what was it you needed again?"

How to set it up for your Wollongong trade business

There are a few ways to get missed call text-back running:

  • Purpose-built CRM platforms (e.g. Go High Level, ServiceM8 + integrations) — include missed call text-back as a built-in feature. Monthly subscription, usually $80–$200/month for the full platform.
  • Custom automation via Make or Zapier — we connect your existing business number to an automation that fires an SMS via Twilio or a similar service. More flexible, lower cost, but requires setup by someone who knows what they're doing.
  • Done-for-you setup — we handle the full configuration, write your message in your voice, test it, and hand it over. Most Illawarra tradies are live within 3-5 business days.

The message itself is critical. A generic corporate-sounding text ("Your call has been received and will be returned within 2 business hours") doesn't convert. Something in your actual voice — using your name, your business name, and language that sounds like you — converts 3-5x better.

Frequently asked questions

What if I don't want customers texting me on my personal phone?

A properly set-up missed call text-back system uses a separate business number (virtual number) for the automated SMS. Replies come into a CRM or dedicated inbox — not your personal phone — so your personal number stays private.

Does it work after hours?

Yes, and after-hours is often where it pays off most. A customer who calls at 7pm on a Friday after seeing your website has probably already decided they want to use you — an immediate text confirming you'll be in touch Monday morning keeps them from starting the search again on the weekend.

Can I turn it off when I can answer the phone?

Most systems only fire if the call isn't answered — so if you pick up, no text is sent. You can also set it to only fire during certain hours, or toggle it on and off manually.

What if the same person calls multiple times?

Good systems deduplicate — if the same number calls twice in a short window, only one text goes out. You can set the deduplication window (e.g. don't send to the same number more than once in 24 hours).

Is it legal to send automated SMS in Australia?

Yes, provided the message is sent in response to an inbound contact (a missed call counts). You're not cold-texting — you're responding to someone who reached out to you first. This is covered under standard business communication guidelines and does not require explicit SMS consent under Australian spam law when it's a transactional/service response.
