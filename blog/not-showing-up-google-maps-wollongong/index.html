Why Your Wollongong Business Isn't Showing Up on Google Maps (And How to Fix It)

  2. 8 reasons you're not showing up

If you've Googled your own business type in Wollongong and can't find yourself — or you're buried on page two while a competitor with a worse reputation sits in the Maps 3-pack — you're not imagining it, and it's not random. There are specific, fixable reasons this happens.

I've audited hundreds of local business profiles across the Illawarra and the same issues come up again and again. The good news: most of them can be fixed without spending a cent on advertising.

How the Google Maps 3-pack actually works

When someone searches "electrician Wollongong" or "café near me", Google decides which three businesses to show in the Maps block using three core signals: relevance (does your listing match what was searched?), distance (how close is the business to the searcher?), and prominence (how well-known and trusted is the business across the web?).

Prominence is the one most businesses can actually influence — and it's driven by reviews, website authority, citations, and how complete and accurate your Google Business Profile is. Distance is mostly fixed; relevance is about category and keywords. This guide focuses on the controllable factors.

8 reasons your Wollongong business isn't showing up on Google Maps

Reason 1

Your Google Business Profile isn't claimed or verified

Google sometimes creates automatic listings for businesses based on publicly available data — but these are unclaimed and often inaccurate. If you haven't manually claimed and verified your listing, you have no control over it and Google won't rank it well. This is the single most common issue we see with Wollongong businesses.

Fix: Go to business.google.com, search for your business, and claim it. Verification typically involves a phone call, SMS code, or postcard sent to your business address. Once verified, fill in every single field.
Reason 2

Your profile is incomplete

Google's algorithm favours complete profiles. If you're missing business hours, services, photos, a description, or your website link, you're being outcompeted by businesses that have filled everything in. Google can't rank what it doesn't understand.

Fix: Log into your Google Business Profile and complete every section — business description (750 characters), all relevant service categories, opening hours, website, phone number, and at least 10 photos (exterior, interior, team, work examples). Add your services with individual descriptions and prices where applicable.
Reason 3

You have too few reviews — or none

Reviews are one of Google's most heavily weighted local ranking signals. A business with 40 reviews rated 4.7 stars will almost always outrank a business with 4 reviews rated 5.0 stars. In competitive Wollongong categories (plumbers, electricians, builders, dentists), the 3-pack occupants typically have 30-100+ reviews.

Fix: Start asking every happy customer for a review immediately after the job. Send a direct link via SMS — don't make them hunt for you on Google. Aim for consistent volume (2-4 per month minimum). See our full guide to getting more Google reviews.
Reason 4

Your business category is wrong or too generic

Your primary category tells Google what searches to consider you relevant for. Using "Contractor" instead of "Plumber" or "Food" instead of "Pizza Restaurant" means you're invisible for the specific searches your customers are actually making.

Fix: In your Google Business Profile, check your primary category against exactly what your customers would search. You can add up to 9 additional categories — use all that are relevant. Avoid being too broad; specificity wins.
Reason 5

Your NAP is inconsistent across the web

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone number. If your business is listed as "XYZ Plumbing" on Google but "XYZ Plumbing Services Pty Ltd" on True Local, or your phone number has different formatting across directories, Google loses confidence in your legitimacy and your local ranking suffers. This is called a citation inconsistency problem.

Fix: Search your business name on Google and audit every directory listing. Your name, address, and phone number must be character-for-character identical everywhere. Prioritise Yelp, True Local, Yellow Pages, Facebook, Hotfrog, and any industry-specific directories.
Reason 6

Your website has no local content

Google cross-references your website with your Google Business Profile. A website that never mentions "Wollongong", "Illawarra", or your suburb gives Google weaker confidence that you genuinely serve the local area. Thin websites — one page, no blog, no local landing pages — consistently underperform in local search.

Fix: Make sure your website mentions Wollongong naturally throughout — in your homepage headline, your about page, your service pages, and ideally in blog content. Add a footer address. Consider dedicated suburb pages if you serve multiple areas (Shellharbour, Kiama, etc.).
Reason 7

Your listing has been suspended

Google suspends listings that violate its guidelines — sometimes legitimately, sometimes incorrectly. A suspended listing is completely invisible in Maps. Common triggers include: keyword stuffing in your business name (e.g. "John's Plumbing — Best Plumber Wollongong"), using a virtual office or PO box as your address, or having multiple duplicate listings.

Fix: Check your Google Business Profile dashboard for any suspension notices. If suspended, appeal via the Google Business Profile Help Centre with documentation (business registration, utility bill, photos of premises). Remove any duplicate listings first. Clean up your business name — it should match your legal trading name only.
Reason 8

You're being outcompeted — and need more prominence

Sometimes your profile is technically complete and correct, but your competitors have simply been doing local SEO longer and more consistently. They have more reviews, more backlinks, more local content, and more citations. In this case, there's no single fix — it's about steady, compounding effort over 3-6 months.

Fix: Commit to a local SEO plan: reviews every week, monthly blog content referencing Wollongong, citation building across 20+ relevant directories, and getting backlinks from local Wollongong websites (Chamber of Commerce, local media, supplier sites). The gap closes faster than most people expect.
📍 The proximity myth

Many business owners assume they don't show up because they're not physically close enough to the searcher. While distance is a factor, it's not the most important one — especially for service-area businesses that travel to customers. A plumber in Fairy Meadow can easily outrank a plumber in Wollongong CBD if their profile is stronger. Focus on controllable factors first.

How to audit your own Google Business Profile in 10 minutes

Run through this checklist right now:

  • Claimed and verified? — Log into business.google.com and check for a blue verification tick.
  • Business name correct? — Must match your legal trading name. No keyword stuffing.
  • Primary category accurate? — Should precisely describe what you do, not a broad parent category.
  • Address and phone consistent with your website? — Character for character.
  • Website linked? — Must point to your actual homepage, not a Facebook page.
  • Business hours set? — Including holiday hours where applicable.
  • 10+ photos? — Real photos, not stock images. Exterior, interior, team, work samples.
  • Business description filled in? — Up to 750 characters. Include your suburb and key services naturally.
  • Services listed with descriptions? — As many as are relevant with individual entries.
  • Reviews present and being responded to? — At least 10+, with recent activity in the last 3 months.

If you score 8 or more of these, your profile issues are unlikely to be the problem — the issue is probably reviews and website authority. If you score fewer than 6, fixing these fundamentals should produce visible Maps improvements within 4-8 weeks.

Realistic timeline to get into the Maps 3-pack

Here's what a typical Wollongong business journey looks like from a neglected or new profile:

  • Week 1-2: Claim, verify, and fully complete the Google Business Profile. Initial Maps visibility usually appears within days.
  • Month 1: First batch of review requests go out. Start publishing local content on your website. Fix any citation inconsistencies.
  • Month 2-3: Reviews accumulate. Local content starts indexing. You begin appearing for lower-competition searches (your suburb + service).
  • Month 3-5: With 20-40 reviews and consistent content, 3-pack appearances for main keywords become realistic in most Wollongong service categories.
  • Month 6+: Compounding effects kick in. Each new review and piece of content strengthens your position. The gap between you and weaker competitors widens.

Frequently asked questions

Why isn't my business showing up on Google Maps even though I've claimed it?

Claiming is just the first step. You also need to complete your profile fully, have consistent NAP data across the web, build reviews, and ensure your website has local content. A claimed but incomplete or low-prominence profile will still rank poorly.

How long does it take to appear in the Google Maps 3-pack?

In most Wollongong service categories, 3-4 months of consistent local SEO effort is enough to crack the 3-pack for at least some keywords. More competitive categories (dentist, plumber, builder) may take 4-6 months. There are no shortcuts that Google won't eventually penalise.

Can I show up in Google Maps without a physical address?

Yes — Google supports Service Area Businesses (SABs) that travel to customers rather than having a shopfront. You can set your service area radius instead of displaying an address. However, you still need to verify with a physical address during setup.

My competitor has a fake address — can I report them?

Yes. You can flag suspicious listings via the "Suggest an edit" option on their Google Business Profile, or submit a report through the Google Business Profile Help Centre. Google investigates these reports, though the timeline varies.
