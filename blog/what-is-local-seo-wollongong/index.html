What Is Local SEO and Why Does Every Wollongong Business Need It?

When someone in Wollongong pulls out their phone and types "plumber near me" or "best café Wollongong", they're doing a local search. The results they see — the map with three business pins at the top — are determined by local SEO.

Local SEO is the process of making your business visible to people searching for what you offer in your area. For almost every Wollongong business that serves local customers, it's the single highest-leverage digital investment you can make.

This guide explains what local SEO is, how it works, and exactly what Illawarra businesses need to do to rank for local searches.

What local SEO actually is

Local SEO (local search engine optimisation) is a set of strategies that improve your business's visibility in Google's local search results. Unlike general SEO — which focuses on ranking for any keyword regardless of location — local SEO specifically targets searches with geographic intent.

These searches fall into two types:

  • Explicit local searches: "plumber Wollongong", "dentist Fairy Meadow", "electrician near Corrimal"
  • Implicit local searches: "emergency plumber", "coffee near me", "builder" — where Google infers the user wants local results based on their location

For implicit local searches, Google uses the device's GPS location to show businesses nearby. This means even when a potential customer doesn't type your suburb, you can still appear — but only if your local SEO is strong enough.

📈 The local search opportunity

"Near me" searches have grown over 500% in the last decade. In the Illawarra, the vast majority of searches for trade services, professional services, health, dining, and retail have local intent. Being in the top 3 local results captures 60-70% of all clicks for that search.

The Google Maps 3-pack — why it matters

When someone searches for a local business or service, Google typically shows a map with three business listings prominently above the organic results. This is called the Local Pack or 3-pack. It's the most valuable real estate in local search.

Studies consistently show that the 3-pack receives more clicks than anything else on the page — including the paid ads above it. For a Wollongong plumber, appearing in the 3-pack for "plumber Wollongong" is equivalent to having a full-page ad in the Yellow Pages used to be — except it's free, and the people clicking have immediate intent to hire.

The 3-pack is powered by your Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business). If you don't have one set up and optimised, you simply cannot appear there — no matter how good your website is.

What Google uses to rank local businesses

Google uses four key factors to determine local rankings:

~30% weight

Relevance

How well your business matches what the searcher is looking for. Categories, services, keywords in your GBP description, and your website all signal relevance.

~30% weight

Distance

How close your business is to the searcher (or the location specified in the search). You can't move your business, but you can optimise for a broader service area.

~40% weight

Prominence

How well-known and trusted your business is. Reviews, ratings, backlinks, citations, and overall online presence all feed into prominence. This is where most of the work happens.

Supporting factor

Behavioural signals

How users interact with your listing — clicks, calls, direction requests, and time on site. Google treats high engagement as a signal that your business is the right result.

Supporting factors

On-page signals

Keywords on your website (especially in title tags, headings, and content), local schema markup, mobile-friendliness, page speed, and NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone number matching across your website and directories).

Local SEO checklist for Wollongong businesses

Here's the practical work that moves the needle for Illawarra businesses:

1. Google Business Profile (GBP)

  • Claim and verify your Google Business Profile at business.google.com
  • Choose the most accurate primary category (e.g. "Plumber" not just "Contractor")
  • Add all relevant secondary categories
  • Fill out every field: hours, services, products, description (use keywords naturally)
  • Upload 10+ high-quality photos of your business, team, and work
  • Add your service area suburbs (Wollongong, Shellharbour, Kiama, etc.)
  • Post weekly GBP updates (offers, news, tips) — signals active management
  • Respond to every review, positive and negative

2. Google reviews

  • Aim for at least 20 reviews to be competitive for most Wollongong keywords
  • Actively ask every happy customer for a Google review (see our full guide on getting more Google reviews)
  • Include keywords naturally in your responses to reviews (Google reads these)
  • Never buy reviews — Google detects and removes them, and risks account suspension

3. NAP consistency

  • Your business Name, Address, and Phone number must be identical everywhere online
  • Check: your website footer, Google Business Profile, Facebook, Yellow Pages, True Local, HealthEngine (if health), Yelp, and any other directories where you're listed
  • Even small discrepancies (e.g. "St" vs "Street") can confuse Google and hurt rankings

4. Local citations

  • Get listed in major Australian directories: Yellow Pages, True Local, Yelp Australia, Hotfrog, StartLocal
  • Get listed in industry-specific directories (hipages, ServiceSeeking for tradies; HealthEngine for health; etc.)
  • Ensure every listing has consistent NAP + website URL

5. Website local SEO

  • Include your suburb and service keywords in your page title tags (e.g. "Plumber Wollongong | Coastal Plumbing")
  • Add your address to your website footer (matching GBP exactly)
  • Create separate service area pages for major suburbs you serve (Shellharbour, Kiama, Dapto, etc.)
  • Add LocalBusiness schema markup (JSON-LD) to your homepage
  • Publish locally relevant content — blog posts about Wollongong-specific topics in your industry

6. Backlinks

  • Get listed on the Wollongong City Council business directory
  • Join the Illawarra Business Chamber and get listed on their site
  • Seek links from local Wollongong news sites, community organisations, and local sponsorships
  • Guest post on local industry blogs or the Illawarra Mercury
⚠ The most common local SEO mistake

Having inconsistent NAP details across the internet. We regularly audit Wollongong businesses and find their Google Business Profile says "(02) 4003 4436" while their website says "0240034436" and their Yellow Pages listing says "(02) 40034436". Google treats these as potentially different businesses and loses trust in your information. Audit all your listings and make them identical.

Local SEO vs regular SEO — what's the difference?

Regular (national/global) SEO targets keywords with no geographic intent — "how to fix a leaking tap", "best accounting software", "tax deductions Australia". These rankings are determined purely by content quality, authority, and technical SEO.

Local SEO targets geographic searches. The ranking algorithm is different, and the tools are different. The biggest difference? Local SEO is heavily influenced by your Google Business Profile and reviews — two things that national SEO barely touches.

For most Wollongong service businesses, local SEO should be the priority. There's no point ranking for "best plumber in Australia" — you want to rank for "plumber Wollongong" and appear in the Maps 3-pack when someone a few kilometres away needs a plumber urgently.

How long does local SEO take?

Local SEO is not instant — but it's faster than people expect when done properly:

  • Weeks 1–4: Google Business Profile optimisation, citation cleanup, and review strategy often produce visible improvements within the first month
  • Months 2–3: New citations and website optimisations start being indexed; rankings begin to move
  • Months 3–6: For most Wollongong industries, competitive 3-pack positions become achievable with consistent work
  • Months 6–12+: Backlink building and content strategy compound — rankings strengthen and become harder for competitors to displace

The key difference from paid ads: local SEO compounds over time. A Google Ads campaign stops generating leads the moment you stop paying. A strong local SEO position keeps generating leads for years, often with minimal ongoing maintenance once established.

Frequently asked questions

Can I do local SEO myself?

Yes — the foundational work (claiming your GBP, ensuring NAP consistency, building basic citations) is something any business owner can do. Where it gets more technical is on-page optimisation, schema markup, and link building. Many Wollongong businesses successfully manage their own GBP and reviews once it's set up properly.

What if I don't have a physical address in Wollongong?

Service-area businesses (tradies, cleaners, mobile services) can still rank in local SEO without a shopfront address. In Google Business Profile, you set a service area rather than displaying your physical address. You can rank for suburbs you service even if you work from home.

How important are reviews for local SEO?

Extremely important — Google reviews are one of the top ranking signals for local search. Businesses with more reviews (and higher average ratings) consistently outrank competitors with similar quality scores. Even more important: businesses with 50+ reviews convert significantly better when potential customers see them in the 3-pack.

Does my website matter for local SEO?

Yes, significantly. Your Google Business Profile can get you into the 3-pack, but your website provides the trust signals (content, structure, backlinks) that help you rank for more competitive terms and in more suburbs. A well-structured, locally-optimised website is a major local SEO asset.

What's the difference between local SEO and Google Business Profile?

Google Business Profile (GBP) is one component of local SEO. Full local SEO includes GBP optimisation, your website, citation building, review strategy, and backlink acquisition. Think of GBP as the centrepiece of your local SEO strategy — not the whole thing.
