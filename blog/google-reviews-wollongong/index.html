How to Get More Google Reviews for Your Wollongong Business (2026 Guide)

Google reviews are the single highest-leverage thing most Wollongong businesses aren't doing consistently. Not because it's hard — it's genuinely not — but because nobody ever showed them a system that takes less than five minutes to set up and runs itself.

If you search "plumber Wollongong" or "electrician near me" right now, the businesses in the Maps 3-pack have one thing in common: they have more reviews, more recent reviews, and better reviews than the competitors just below them. Reviews are both a ranking signal and a conversion signal — they get you found and they get you chosen.

This guide gives you the exact process we use with our clients to build a steady flow of 5-star reviews without pestering customers or doing anything Google doesn't allow.

Why Google reviews matter for local SEO

Google uses reviews as a core ranking signal for local search. More specifically, it looks at three things: the quantity of your reviews, the recency (a review from last week carries more weight than one from 2022), and the quality (star rating, keyword-rich content, and whether you respond).

Beyond rankings, reviews drive conversion. Studies consistently show that businesses with 50+ reviews see significantly higher click-through rates from search results than those with fewer than 10. And a business with a 4.8-star rating on 80 reviews will win the click over a 4.9 on 6 reviews almost every time — because volume signals legitimacy.

For Wollongong businesses specifically, reviews are even more powerful than in major metros because the local population is smaller and more word-of-mouth driven. A Wollongong resident will often check Google reviews the way they'd check a personal recommendation — they're looking for social proof from people in their own community.

📈 Local ranking factor

In competitive Wollongong service categories (plumbers, electricians, builders, cleaners), the difference between the #1 and #4 spot in Google Maps is typically 20-40 more reviews — not years of SEO work. Reviews are the fastest lever you can pull.

The 3 biggest mistakes Wollongong businesses make with reviews

1. Asking too late (or not at all)

Most businesses that do ask for reviews do it weeks after the job — when the customer has already moved on mentally and the emotional high of a great experience has faded. The best time to ask is within 24 hours of completing the job, while the experience is fresh. Same day is even better.

2. Making it too hard

Telling a customer "just Google us and leave a review" is asking them to do five steps: open Google, search your business name, find the right listing, click Write a Review, and compose something. Most won't bother. Send them a direct link that opens the review form in one tap.

3. Asking inconsistently

Asking 12 customers in one week and then nobody for two months doesn't work. Google's algorithm favours a steady drip of recent reviews over irregular spikes. You need a system, not a campaign.

When and how to ask for reviews

The golden rule: ask when the customer is at their happiest. That's almost always immediately after the job is done and they've seen the result. For service businesses, that's the moment the plumber is packing up their tools, the cleaner is doing the final walkthrough, or the invoice is sent.

The best channel is SMS. Email open rates for review requests hover around 20-30%. SMS open rates are 95%+. If you only have an email address, use it — but prioritise getting mobile numbers from customers.

1
Get your Google review shortlink

In your Google Business Profile dashboard, click "Get more reviews" to copy your unique review link. It looks like g.page/yourbusiness/review. Save this somewhere you can access it quickly.

2
Create a review request message

Write a short, genuine SMS message (see templates below). Keep it under 160 characters if possible so it sends as a single message.

3
Send within 24 hours of job completion

Set a phone reminder or use automation (see below). Don't leave it until the end of the week — the emotional window closes fast.

4
Follow up once if no response

A single follow-up 3-5 days later is fine and increases conversion significantly. Don't send more than two messages — that crosses the line into pestering.

Review request templates that actually work

The best review requests are short, personal, and make it clear exactly what you're asking for and why. Here are templates proven to work for Wollongong service businesses:

📱 SMS — Tradie / Service Business

Hi [Name], it was great working with you today! If you're happy with the [job type], I'd really appreciate a quick Google review — it helps local families find us. Here's the link: [your review link]. Thanks, [Your name]

📱 SMS — Retail / Hospitality

Hi [Name], thanks for coming in today! If you enjoyed your experience, we'd love a Google review — it means a lot for a local business like ours. One tap: [your review link] 🙏

📱 SMS — Professional Services (accountant, lawyer, consultant)

Hi [Name], it was a pleasure helping you with [matter]. When you have a moment, a Google review makes a real difference for our practice. Link: [your review link]. No pressure at all — thank you for your trust.

✉ Email subject line

Subject: Quick favour — would you leave us a Google review?

Hi [Name], I hope everything is going well with [result/project]. I have a small favour to ask — if you were happy with our work, would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It takes about 60 seconds and genuinely helps other Wollongong families or businesses find us. You can do it here: [link]. Thank you so much. — [Your name]

⚠ What NOT to do

Don't offer discounts, freebies, or any incentive in exchange for reviews — this violates Google's policies and can get your listing penalised or suspended. Don't ask only happy customers (called "review gating") — if you're going to ask, ask everyone consistently. And never write fake reviews or pay for them. Google's detection is sophisticated and the penalties are severe.

How to respond to every review

Responding to reviews is a confirmed local ranking signal — and the vast majority of Wollongong businesses don't do it. This is an easy win.

Responding to positive reviews

Thank the customer personally, mention the specific job or service (Google picks up keywords in your responses), and invite them back or wish them well. Keep it genuine, not formulaic.

⭐ Positive review response

Thanks so much for the kind words, [Name]! It was a pleasure getting your [specific job] sorted — glad we could help. If you ever need anything else, don't hesitate to reach out. — Ava and the team

Responding to negative reviews

Take a breath before responding. Never argue, never get defensive, and never post anything that sounds like you're trying to discredit the reviewer. Your response is read by every future potential customer — you want to demonstrate that you take feedback seriously and handle issues with professionalism.

😱 Negative review response

Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to share your feedback — I'm really sorry your experience didn't meet the standard we aim for. I'd like to make this right. Please call me directly on (02) 4003 4436 so we can discuss it. — Ava Webster

💡 Pro tip

Set up a Google Business Profile email notification so you're alerted the moment a new review is posted. Aim to respond within 24 hours — both for ranking reasons and because a fast response on a negative review can sometimes prompt the reviewer to update their rating.

How to automate your review collection

If asking for reviews manually sounds like something that'll fall through the cracks — it will, for everyone. The businesses consistently accumulating reviews have automated the process.

The simplest approach is a missed call text-back + job completion trigger built with a tool like Make (formerly Integromat) or a purpose-built review automation platform. When a job is marked complete in your CRM or booking system, it automatically fires an SMS to the customer's number with your review link — no manual step required.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get a Google review link to send to customers?

Log into your Google Business Profile, go to the 'Get more reviews' section, and copy your review shortlink. Send this link directly in SMS or email — it opens the review form in one tap.

Can I ask customers for Google reviews?

Yes. Google allows you to ask any customer for a review. What's not allowed is offering incentives (discounts, gifts) or selectively asking only happy customers. A genuine ask to any customer after a completed job is within Google's guidelines.

How many reviews do I need to rank in the Maps 3-pack in Wollongong?

In most Wollongong service categories, 15–25 recent reviews puts you in serious contention. More important than raw volume is recency — recent reviews signal that your business is active and well-regarded right now.

Should I respond to every Google review?

Yes. Responding to reviews is a confirmed local ranking signal and takes less than a minute per review. Aim to respond to every review — positive and negative — within 24 hours.

What happens if I get a fake negative review?

You can flag the review for removal via your Google Business Profile dashboard. Google will investigate. In the meantime, respond professionally and calmly — don't accuse the reviewer of being fake in your public response. If the review violates Google's policies (no genuine customer experience, spam, etc.) it will usually be removed within 2–4 weeks.
