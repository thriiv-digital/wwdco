Tradie Websites: What Actually Works (And What Wastes Your Money)

Most tradie websites share the same problem: they look fine, but they don't actually win jobs. They exist as digital business cards — visited briefly, ignored, and abandoned for a competitor with a cleaner quote form or more Google reviews.

The tradies we work with in Wollongong and the Illawarra didn't get burned by ugly websites. They got burned by decent-looking websites that weren't designed to convert — to actually turn a visitor into a phone call, a form submission, or a booked job.

This guide is about what separates a tradie website that generates a steady stream of enquiries from one that sits quietly in the corner costing $50/month hosting.

Why most tradie websites don't actually win jobs

We've reviewed hundreds of small business websites. The single most common failure isn't design — it's focus. Most tradie websites are written about the tradie, not for the customer.

"Family-owned business. 20 years of experience. We take pride in our work."

These statements mean nothing to someone who just found water pooling under their kitchen sink. They want to know: Are you available now? How quickly can you come? Can I trust you? How do I get a quote?

A website that doesn't answer those questions immediately — in the first 5 seconds — loses the visitor. On mobile, you have even less time.

📸 The 5-second test

Pull up your website on your phone. In 5 seconds, can a visitor tell: (1) exactly what you do, (2) which suburbs you cover, (3) how to contact you right now, and (4) whether they can trust you — your Google rating, number of reviews, and any licences or certifications? If not, you're losing jobs before they start.

What a high-converting tradie website actually needs

A headline that speaks to the customer's need

Not "Welcome to Coastal Plumbing" — that tells a visitor nothing. Instead: "Wollongong's fastest emergency plumber — available 7 days, arrive in 60 minutes." Every word does work. It names the location, addresses urgency, signals availability, and sets a concrete expectation.

A phone number that's impossible to miss

Your phone number should appear in the top-right of every page — as a clickable link on mobile. If a potential customer has to search your website for your contact details, you've already lost them. For trade businesses where emergency calls are common, a sticky "Call Now" button on mobile can double your call rate.

Trust signals above the fold

Before scrolling, a visitor should see evidence they can trust you:

  • Your Google star rating and number of reviews (link to your Google Business Profile)
  • Licensing or certification logos (QBCC, Fair Trading, Master Electricians, etc.)
  • A real photo of you or your team — not stock photos
  • Years in business and the suburbs you service

A simple, friction-free quote form

The best tradie quote forms ask for 3–4 fields maximum: name, phone, suburb, and a brief description of the job. That's it. Every extra field reduces your completion rate. Don't ask for email if you're going to call them back anyway. Don't ask for a full address before the first conversation.

Real photos of your work

Nothing builds trust faster than genuine before/after photos of completed jobs. Customers looking for a Wollongong tradie are comparing you against competitors — real job photos are a powerful differentiator against competitors who rely on stock imagery. Even 10–15 high-quality photos of your actual work can transform conversion rates.

Clear service pages with suburb-specific content

Each service you offer should have its own page. "Electrical services" is too vague. "Switchboard upgrades Wollongong", "Emergency electrician Wollongong", "Solar installation Illawarra" — these are specific pages that rank for specific searches and speak directly to what the customer needs.

📈 The suburb page strategy

If you service Wollongong, Shellharbour, Kiama, and Nowra, create a separate page for each suburb. "Plumber Shellharbour", "Plumber Kiama" — these are distinct searches with much lower competition than "plumber Wollongong". Suburb pages are one of the highest-ROI things a tradie website can have.

What wastes money on tradie websites

Worth investing in

  • Professional copywriting focused on conversion
  • Real photography of your work and team
  • Google Business Profile optimisation
  • Fast mobile loading speed
  • Service-specific and suburb-specific pages
  • Schema markup for local SEO
  • Missed call text-back automation
  • Google review strategy

Often a waste of money

  • Fancy animations and parallax effects
  • Stock photography (use real photos)
  • A blog you'll never write for
  • Pop-ups and chat widgets on every page
  • Social media feeds embedded on the homepage
  • Complicated booking systems for simple quote requests
  • Full-screen video backgrounds
  • Countdown timers and fake urgency tactics

The biggest money waster: a website without local SEO

The most common complaint we hear from tradies is "I got a website built for $3,000 and got zero leads from it." In almost every case, the problem wasn't the design — it was that the website had no local SEO strategy. No keyword research. No suburb pages. No Google Business Profile integration. No schema markup. A beautiful website that Google can't find is a brochure that never gets delivered.

The pages every tradie website needs

  • Homepage — your most important page. Headline, trust signals, services overview, featured reviews, and a strong CTA
  • Service pages — one per major service, written with keywords customers actually search for
  • About page — your story, your team, your qualifications. This is where customers decide if they trust you
  • Gallery / portfolio — real photos of completed jobs. Before and after where possible
  • Testimonials / reviews — pull Google reviews onto your site. Social proof is one of the most powerful conversion tools available
  • Contact / quote page — simple form, phone number prominent, map if you have a physical location
  • Suburb pages — one per major area you service. Thin content is fine if it's genuinely relevant to that suburb
  • FAQs — answer the questions customers actually ask. Also excellent for voice search and featured snippets

Local SEO: the engine behind your tradie website

A tradie website without local SEO is a car without an engine. It might look great in the driveway but it's not going anywhere.

Local SEO is what gets your website (and your Google Business Profile) in front of Wollongong customers when they search for what you do. The mechanics are well-understood: it's a combination of keyword-optimised pages, consistent business information across the web, Google reviews, and local backlinks.

For Illawarra tradies specifically, the opportunity is significant. Many local trade businesses have weak or non-existent local SEO, which means a tradie who invests properly in this area can dominate local search results for years.

The three things that move the needle fastest for tradie local SEO:

  1. Google Business Profile — claimed, fully completed, actively managed
  2. Google reviews — consistently asking happy customers; responding to all reviews
  3. Suburb pages — specific pages for each area you service with genuine, keyword-rich content

WordPress vs website builders vs custom build

Website builders (Wix, Squarespace, Webflow)

Easy to set up, reasonable design options, monthly subscription cost ($25–$50/month). The main downside is limited control over technical SEO, slower page speeds than custom-built sites, and less flexibility for complex suburb page strategies. Good for a tradie who needs something basic and doesn't want to deal with hosting and maintenance.

WordPress

The most widely used platform for tradie websites in Australia. Highly flexible, excellent SEO control, huge ecosystem of plugins. Requires more technical management (hosting, updates, security). Generally $2,500–$5,000 to have built professionally, then $50–$150/month in hosting and maintenance.

Custom coded

Fastest loading, most flexibility, no ongoing platform licensing. Higher upfront cost ($4,000–$10,000+) but no monthly platform fees. Best for tradies who want maximum SEO performance and a completely unique site. This is what we build at Wollongong Web Design Co — hand-coded, no page builder bloat, extraordinarily fast.

💡 The platform matters less than you think

The biggest factor in whether your tradie website wins jobs is not the platform — it's the strategy. Keyword research, suburb targeting, copywriting, and local SEO are what determine your results. A well-built WordPress site outperforms a poorly-thought-out custom build every time.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to build a tradie website?

A professionally built tradie website typically takes 3–6 weeks from kickoff to launch. This includes discovery, copywriting, design, development, and any photography. DIY website builders can be live in a day or two, but the strategy and content work takes longer regardless of platform.

Do I need to update my website regularly?

Your core pages (services, about, contact) don't need frequent updating. What benefits from regular activity is your Google Business Profile (weekly posts) and, if you have one, a blog. But for most tradies, a well-built static site with consistent Google Business Profile management delivers excellent results without constant content updates.

Can I get leads from my website without Google Ads?

Yes — through local SEO. Local SEO generates free organic traffic from Google searches. It takes 3–6 months to build, but once established it delivers leads at effectively zero ongoing cost. Many Wollongong tradies with strong local SEO get 10–30+ enquiries per month without spending a dollar on advertising.

Should I have a quote form or just a phone number?

Both. Different customers prefer different channels — some will fill out a form at 10pm when they can't call, others won't fill out any form and will only call. Make both options extremely visible. For urgent jobs (emergency trade work), a phone number with a "Call Now" tap-to-call button on mobile is especially critical.

What's the biggest mistake tradies make when they get a new website?

Treating it as a one-time purchase rather than an ongoing asset. The website launch is the beginning, not the end. The tradies who get the most from their websites consistently ask for Google reviews, keep their Google Business Profile active, and occasionally update their service pages with new content. The ones who "set and forget" typically get disappointing results within 12 months.
