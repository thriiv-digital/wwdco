The Problem with "Set and Forget"

What Happens When No One Is Looking After Your Website

Most Wollongong businesses invest in a professional website launch and then… nothing. The site sits there, updating itself occasionally (or not at all), accumulating outdated plugins, and slowly drifting away from the security baseline it was built to.

This is how websites get hacked. Not in dramatic Hollywood fashion — but quietly, through an unpatched vulnerability in a plugin that the developer stopped maintaining six months ago. A piece of malware gets injected. Google starts showing a "this site may be harmful" warning. The site gets blacklisted. Customers can't get through. Revenue stops.

The average cost of recovering from a WordPress hack — including malware removal, security audit, reputation repair, and potential Google penalty recovery — is several thousand dollars and days of downtime. The average cost of a maintenance plan that would have prevented it is $149 to $299 per month.

Beyond security, unmaintained sites get slower over time. Hosting providers change infrastructure. PHP versions become incompatible. Google updates its Core Web Vitals requirements and your PageSpeed score drops 30 points overnight. None of these things announce themselves — they just quietly cost you rankings and conversions until someone notices.

A maintenance care plan is not optional for any Wollongong business that relies on its website for leads, enquiries, eCommerce revenue, or credibility. It's the cost of keeping a business asset in working order — exactly the same as servicing a vehicle you depend on.

Security

Security Monitoring & Patching

Security maintenance is the most critical thing we do for your website, and the one most businesses either neglect entirely or handle reactively — after a breach has already occurred. Our approach is systematic and proactive: we monitor vulnerability databases, apply patches as they're released, and scan your site regularly to catch any indicators of compromise before they escalate.

For WordPress sites, this means monitoring all installed plugins and themes against known CVE databases, applying security patches as soon as they're released (after staging testing), updating WordPress core on every new version, and running weekly malware scans using professional scanning tools. We also harden your WordPress installation against common attack vectors — login brute-force protection, file permission lockdown, XML-RPC disable, and security headers configuration.

For Shopify sites, security is largely managed by Shopify's infrastructure, but we monitor your installed apps for known issues, review app permissions regularly, and advise on third-party scripts that may introduce vulnerabilities.

Security service includes
  • Weekly automated malware and vulnerability scans
  • Plugin & theme security patching (staged)
  • WordPress core updates
  • Login security hardening (2FA, brute-force protection)
  • Security headers configuration
  • SSL certificate monitoring and renewal
  • Google Safe Browsing status monitoring
💾
Backups

Daily Backups & Disaster Recovery

Backups are insurance — you don't think about them until you need them, and then you really need them. We take daily encrypted backups of your full website: files, database, media library, and configuration. Backups are stored offsite (separate from your hosting server) with 30-day retention, so a server-level incident can't take your backups down with your site.

The backup is only half the service. The other half is knowing we can actually restore from it when it matters. We test restores quarterly to confirm backups are valid and complete — not just a backup notification that turns out to contain an empty archive. For Premium plan clients, we maintain a permanent staging environment that mirrors your live site, so restore testing is fast and confident.

For eCommerce clients, order data is included in backups, with special attention to WooCommerce order tables and Shopify export schedules. We can restore to any point in the last 30 days — typically within an hour for smaller sites, two to four hours for larger ones.

Backup service includes
  • Daily full-site backups (files + database)
  • Encrypted offsite storage
  • 30-day backup retention
  • Quarterly restore testing
  • Emergency restore on demand
  • Pre-update snapshot before every significant change

Uptime

Uptime Monitoring & Incident Response

We monitor your site every minute, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. If your site goes down, we receive an alert within 60 seconds — at any time of day or night. We investigate the cause, contact your hosting provider if needed, and aim to have your site back online before most visitors even notice there was an issue.

We also monitor your SSL certificate expiry (a lapsed SSL causes browser security warnings and immediate trust damage), your domain name expiry, and key page response times. Many hosting providers give you basic uptime monitoring, but their alerts typically trigger after several minutes of downtime and require you to act. We handle the response — you get a notification summary once it's resolved.

Monthly uptime reports are included in every care plan, showing your site's availability percentage, any incidents that occurred, response times, and how we resolved them. Transparent accountability with a clear record of what we've been doing on your behalf.

Monitoring includes

Performance

Speed Optimisation & Core Web Vitals

Website speed is a ranking factor for Google and a direct driver of conversion rates. Research consistently shows that a one-second improvement in page load time increases conversions by 7–12%. The inverse is also true — slow sites lose customers, often silently, before you can measure the loss. Google's Core Web Vitals update made speed performance even more directly tied to search rankings.

We include monthly PageSpeed audits in every care plan, tracking your Core Web Vitals scores (LCP, FID, CLS) over time and addressing any degradation before it affects your rankings. For WordPress sites, performance maintenance includes database optimisation, image compression on new uploads, cache management, and CDN monitoring. For Shopify sites, we monitor app load impact and advise on script optimisation.

Performance can also be affected by hosting issues — overcrowded shared servers, PHP version changes, or memory limit adjustments. We monitor and investigate these at the server level and advise on hosting upgrades when your current plan is limiting performance.

Performance service includes
  • Monthly PageSpeed & Core Web Vitals audit
  • Database cleanup and optimisation
  • Image optimisation on new uploads
  • Cache management
  • CDN performance monitoring
  • Hosting performance review

FAQ

Website Maintenance Wollongong — Common Questions

Do I have to be a Wollongong Web Design Co client to get a care plan?
▾
No. We maintain WordPress and Shopify sites for Wollongong businesses regardless of who built them. We start with a site audit to understand its current state, then onboard it to our monitoring and backup systems. There's a one-off onboarding fee of $199 for sites we didn't build, which covers the audit, security baseline review, and initial setup.
What platforms do you maintain?
▾
We specialise in WordPress (including WooCommerce) and Shopify. These cover the vast majority of Wollongong business websites. We do not currently offer maintenance plans for Squarespace, Wix, or custom-coded sites outside our own builds.
Are there lock-in contracts?
▾
No lock-in contracts. All plans are month-to-month, billed monthly. Cancel any time with 30 days written notice. If you cancel, we'll provide a full handover document with all access credentials and a summary of your site's current state.
What if my site gets hacked while on a care plan?
▾
We handle malware removal and site restoration at no additional cost for Professional and Premium plan clients, provided the breach was through a vector we're responsible for monitoring (outdated plugin, theme, or WordPress core). We restore from the most recent clean backup, remove any injected malware, and harden against the attack vector. Essential plan clients receive malware removal at a discounted hourly rate.
What counts as a "content update" in the Professional and Premium plans?
▾
Content updates are small, defined changes to existing pages: text edits, image swaps, adding a new team member, updating contact details, adding a product or portfolio item. They don't include new page builds, custom development, or design changes — those are quoted separately. The 30-minute or 60-minute credit is development time; a typical text change takes 15–20 minutes including QA and testing.
How quickly can you restore my site from backup?
▾
For most sites (under 5GB), we can restore a backup within 1–2 hours of the decision to restore. For larger sites or complex database-heavy WooCommerce stores, allow 2–4 hours. We will always confirm the target restore point with you before restoring, especially for eCommerce sites where recent orders may exist in the live database but not in a backup from earlier that day.
Do you maintain hosting, or just the website?
▾
We maintain the website application layer — WordPress, plugins, themes, database, and files. If you're hosted with us, we also manage the hosting infrastructure. If you're on external hosting (SiteGround, Kinsta, WP Engine, etc.), we work within that environment and liaise with your hosting provider on server-level issues. We're platform-agnostic and have experience with all major Australian and international WordPress hosts.
How do I get started?
▾
Fill out the form below or call us on (02) 4003 4436. We'll ask for basic details about your site (platform, hosting, current plugins), do a free site health check, and recommend the right plan. Onboarding typically takes 2–3 business days — we set up monitoring, run the first backup, apply any outstanding updates (on staging first), and send you a confirmation report. You're protected from day one.
